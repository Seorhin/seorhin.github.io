Research

My current research focuses on understanding the quantum level structure of lanthanide atoms on surfaces. I use multiplet calculations, crystal-field and ligand-field models, and experimental constraints from XAS, XMCD, and ESR-STM measurements.

Previous Research Experience

Before my current work, I gained experience in an ion-trap quantum computing laboratory, where I worked on AOM and PID control systems, and in an STM laboratory, where I assisted surface preparation and basic STM/STS measurements.

Projects & Notes

I am also interested in conceptual questions in quantum physics, including the role of paths, braiding, and topology in understanding quantum states and particle exchange.

Teaching & Outreach

I have experience in academic mentoring and science communication. I am also developing outreach materials, including a board-game-based introduction to STM and atomic-scale quantum systems.
